Postcard Theory of Digital Scarcity is a project that explores the tension between constructed scarcity and infinite reproducibility in the digital age.

The postcards were generated entirely within the internet, never existing as physical objects until their transformation into printed artifacts. Their uniqueness is established through the authoritative stamp of the postal service and the official date marking, granting them a singular status.

As materialized fragments of network interactions—combining map screenshots, digital postal services, and online-generated imagery—these artworks initially embody a rare, physical presence. However, their subsequent digital archiving disrupts this material exclusivity, returning them to the realm of digital ubiquity and infinite circulation on the internet.
